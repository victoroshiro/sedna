Mediterranean

We offer bareboat yacht charter in thirteen different Mediterranean locations within Greece, Italy, Croatia and Turkey.
Between them, these destinations deliver a choice of Mediterranean sailing holiday to suit all levels of sailing expertise and confidence, from protected waters and short passages to exhilarating open-water crossings.

A Mediterranean yacht charter also affords a unique opportunity to experience a wide diversity of cultures through the spectacular scenery, ancient history and amazing food that are to be encountered along every coastline.

And whether the priority is quaint fishing villages and deserted beaches or lively towns and exciting nightlife, we have the perfect Mediterranean bareboat yacht charter for every taste.

An intoxicating fusion of cultures and civilisations, Sicily with its stunning natural splendour provides the perfect backdrop for a charter around its waters.

Athens yacht charter

The challenging sailing around the Cyclades Islands to the west is a great sail from Athens which is located in the more sheltered waters of the Saronic Gulf.

The famous Meltemi northerly winds and sheer number of islands make for some exhilirating sailing. The islands include Mykonos, Syros and Santorini and give you nightlife, seclusion and rugged beauty all within close proximity.

Dubrovnik yacht charter

The wondrous medieval walled city of Dubrovnik old town is an appropriate gateway to the Dalmatian coastline.

This world famous sailing area, combines history and culture with stunning natural beauty. The steady winds transport you between lively historic towns and secluded bays. Bisevo’s famous blue grotto and the beautiful island of Korcula are two of the must see attractions.

Dalmatian Islands yacht charter

You can sail out from our base at Agana and head south to the beautiful islands of Brac, Hvar and Korcula, with its bustling vibrant culture or head north to the Kornati archipelago, with its more serene sailing and outstanding natural beauty.

Either way you will have a sailing experience to remember, but you could always do both and book two weeks or come back next year!
